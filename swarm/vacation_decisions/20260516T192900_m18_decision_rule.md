# Vacation Decision: M18 Decision Rule

## Situation

The 48-hour and 72-hour vacation-mode instructions authorize M18 joint
KV-cache + activation protection and specify its core regimes, but they do
not give exact pass/kill thresholds. The swarm is instructed to decide rather
than wait when a missing detail blocks progress.

## Options Considered

1. Reuse the M11/M17 positive-method bar exactly: median recovery at least
   `0.30`, CI lower above `0.10`, and clear separation from controls.
2. Use the older M2/M10 bar: median recovery at least `0.40`, CI lower above
   `0.20`.
3. Treat M18 as descriptive only with no pass/kill decision.

## Decision

I chose option 1. M18 is part of the post-M2/M10 smooth/coupled method sprint,
where M11 and M17 use the `0.30` median and `0.10` CI-lower positive-method
threshold. I added two M18-specific control requirements:

- beat the KIVI-style key-only baseline by at least `0.10` median recovery;
- beat the random-coupled activation+K control by at least `0.20` median
  recovery.

I also made `activation+K` the primary gate and `activation+K+V` a secondary
diagnostic arm that cannot rescue a failed primary gate. This avoids a
post-hoc positive claim if the K-only joint hypothesis fails but a broader
KV arm happens to improve.

## Why This Preserves the Scientific Question

M18 asks whether activation channel evidence becomes useful only when coupled
to the corresponding key-cache channel. The chosen thresholds preserve the
same positive-method bar used by adjacent Phase 9 methods while requiring
M18 to beat both a KV-only baseline and a random-channel control.

## What Would Invalidate This Decision

If the human intended M18 to be descriptive-only, or intended the K+V arm to
be a co-primary pass condition, the preregistration should be reviewed before
running M18. No M18 runner has been invoked at the time this decision file is
authored.
