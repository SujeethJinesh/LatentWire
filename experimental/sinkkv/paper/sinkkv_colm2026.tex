\begin{abstract}
This is a living paper shell for a preregistered Mac-first experiment. SinkKV
tests whether protecting the first sink K/V positions while quantizing the rest
of the KV cache recovers the quality loss of uniform FP4-style KV quantization.
No positive result, GPU throughput, latency, HBM, or native FP4 claim is made
yet.
\end{abstract}

\section{Current Gate}

The preregistered Mac gate compares BF16 K/V, uniform simulated MXFP4 K/V, and
sink-protected simulated MXFP4 K/V. Promotion requires sink protection to recover
at least half of the uniform low-precision quality gap on at least two
model/length surfaces, with confidence intervals excluding the no-recovery
boundary.

\section{Claim Boundary}

Allowed now: a preregistered method hypothesis and deterministic local
simulation utilities. Not allowed: native FP4, vLLM integration, speed, memory,
HBM, or benchmark-quality claims.
