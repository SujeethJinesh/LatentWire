# Recent Refinement, Quantization, and Connector References for LatentWire

Web check: 2026-04-21. This memo collects recent 2025-2026 references that are most likely to produce positive-method LatentWire ablations. The focus is cross-model communication under token, byte, KV, and coordinate mismatch: multimodal connector/projector design, latent diffusion or flow-style iterative refinement, universal feature dictionaries and model stitching, tokenizer/vocabulary adaptation, outlier-aware quantization and KV compression, and latent multi-agent competitors.

## Primary sources

- **[Latent Collaboration in Multi-Agent Systems / LatentMAS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20639)** and **[LatentMAS code](https://github.com/Gen-Verse/LatentMAS)**. `Core idea:` multi-agent systems can communicate directly in latent space and report accuracy, output-token, and end-to-end latency gains over text-mediated MAS baselines. `LatentWire use:` treat LatentMAS as the closest competitor/benchmark bootstrap, not just related work. `Ablation hook:` run comparable single-model, text-MAS, and latent-channel controls on math/science/code subsets while logging accuracy per output token and accuracy per wall-clock second. `Interpretability hook:` require route-level traces and verifier-localized help/harm because latent channels are harder to audit than text.

- **[SEMI-style shared projector / modality adapter synthesis](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/unified-sequence-modeling-with-modality-specific-adapters)** and **[Cross-Modality Projector survey note](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/cross-modality-projector-mlp)**. `Core idea:` multimodal LLMs often isolate modality-specific encoders from a shared LLM through small projectors, LoRA adapters, resamplers, or routed connector banks. `LatentWire use:` copy the connector pattern for model-to-model communication: frozen source/target, small bridge, optional routed projector bank. `Ablation hook:` compare one monolithic bridge against a bank of low-rank projectors routed by query type, source layer, target layer, or confidence. `Interpretability hook:` log selected projector ID, gate entropy, route stability, per-regime gain, and whether the bridge collapses to one expert.

- **[Universal Sparse Autoencoders: Interpretable Cross-Model Concept Alignment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.03714)** and **[Sparse Autoencoders Reveal Universal Feature Spaces Across Large Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.06981)**. `Core idea:` shared sparse dictionaries can expose cross-model concept bases and make latent overlap measurable. `LatentWire use:` replace opaque coordinate bridges with feature-level transport and feature-level protection. `Ablation hook:` universal-dictionary selector vs raw activation, quant-error, and exact-patch selectors. `Interpretability hook:` log active feature IDs, cross-model feature overlap, reconstruction error, dead-feature rate, and feature-level help/harm.

- **[Transferring Features Across Language Models With Model Stitching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06609)**. `Core idea:` feature transfer can work through relatively small affine stitch maps between model residual streams. `LatentWire use:` use stitching as initialization and as a negative control for whether we need a richer communication mechanism. `Ablation hook:` frozen stitch, stitch-initialized trainable bridge, stitch plus residual repair, and stitch plus protected-frontier quantization. `Interpretability hook:` log stitch residual, CKA/SVCCA alignment, target-side repair gain, and where affine transfer saturates.

- **[LaDiR: Latent Diffusion Enhances LLMs for Text Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04573)**, **[FS-DFM: Fast and Accurate Long Text Generation with Few-Step Diffusion Language Models](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.20624)**, **[On the Role of Discreteness in Diffusion LLMs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.22630)**, and **[Progressive Refinement Regulation for Accelerating Diffusion Language Model Decoding](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04514)**. `Core idea:` latent or discrete diffusion/flow methods trade one-shot autoregressive commitment for iterative refinement. `LatentWire use:` make cross-model communication a repairable latent state, not a single irreversible projection. `Ablation hook:` one-pass bridge vs two-to-four refinement steps, verifier-guided refinement, and adaptive stopping on uncertainty. `Interpretability hook:` log per-step score drift, corrected atoms, newly introduced harm, refinement stop reason, and gain per extra token/byte.

- **[TokAlign: Efficient Vocabulary Adaptation via Token Alignment](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03523)**, **[Cross-Tokenizer Distillation via Approximate Likelihood Matching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.20083)**, **[Enhancing Cross-Tokenizer Knowledge Distillation with Aligned Logits](https://aclanthology.org/2025.findings-acl.419/)**, and **[AdaptiVocab](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19693)**. `Core idea:` tokenizer mismatch can be handled by vocabulary alignment, approximate likelihood matching, aligned-logit distillation, or lightweight vocabulary replacement. `LatentWire use:` treat tokenizer/vocab mismatch as a first-class blocker rather than noise around the bridge. `Ablation hook:` token-ID bridge vs byte-span bridge vs TokAlign-style remap vs adaptive-vocab compression before latent exchange. `Interpretability hook:` log remap purity, byte-span boundary F1, token shrink ratio, OOV/fragmentation rate, and math-token recovery.

- **[RotateKV: Accurate and Robust 2-Bit KV Cache Quantization via Outlier-Aware Adaptive Rotations](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.16383)** and **[IJCAI 2025 proceedings page](https://www.ijcai.org/proceedings/2025/690)**. `Core idea:` channel reordering, pre-RoPE grouped-head rotation, and attention-sink-aware protection can make very low-bit KV quantization less brittle. `LatentWire use:` the protected frontier should probably combine quant-error, sink-token, and pre-RoPE/head-local geometry rather than flat top-k activation. `Ablation hook:` quant-error protection vs sink-aware protection vs grouped-head rotation before transport. `Interpretability hook:` log protected sink tokens, outlier channels, head group, rotation family, and patch-effect correlation.

- **[SpinQuant: LLM Quantization with Learned Rotations](https://proceedings.iclr.cc/paper_files/paper/2025/hash/e5b1c0d4866f72393c522c8a00eed4eb-Abstract-Conference.html)**, **[KurTail: Kurtosis-based LLM Quantization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.01483)**, **[QuaRot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.00456)**, and **[AWQ](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.00978)**. `Core idea:` outliers should be smoothed, rotated, protected, or assigned more precision based on activation-aware statistics rather than uniform precision. `LatentWire use:` bridge compression should use mixed precision and coordinate canonicalization instead of a single bitwidth. `Ablation hook:` uniform bridge quantization vs activation-aware protected channels vs learned/Hadamard/kurtosis rotations vs mixed-bit frontier allocation. `Interpretability hook:` log bit allocation by channel/head/atom, activation kurtosis, error concentration, and whether protected channels match exact patch effects.

- **[KVLinC: KV Cache Quantization with Hadamard Rotation and Linear Correction](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.05373)**, **[Accurate KV Cache Quantization with Outlier Tokens Tracing](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.631/)**, **[TurboQuant / Online Vector Quantization with Near-optimal Distortion Rate](https://openreview.net/attachment?id=tO3ASKZlok&name=pdf)**, **[Google Research TurboQuant blog](https://research.google/blog/turboquant-redefining-ai-efficiency-with-extreme-compression/)**, and **[TurboAngle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27467)**. `Core idea:` modern KV compression stacks rotations, outlier tracing, lightweight correction, and near-lossless vector quantization. `LatentWire use:` a positive method can be framed as transport plus small correction plus allocation, not a single learned adapter. `Ablation hook:` bridge-only vs bridge plus linear correction; uniform scalar quantization vs vector quantization; K-only, V-only, and asymmetric K/V precision. `Interpretability hook:` log correction norm, residual bias, K/V asymmetry, vector-code usage, and retrieval vs reasoning failure split.

- **[ExLlamaV2 / EXL2 mixed-bit format](https://github.com/SinanAkkoyun/exllama-v2)** and **[EXL2-for-all](https://github.com/chu-tianxiang/exl2-for-all)**. `Core idea:` practical mixed-bit quantization can hit a target average bits-per-weight by allocating precision nonuniformly. `LatentWire use:` copy the engineering prior: a bridge should spend bits on fragile atoms/channels and starve redundant ones. `Ablation hook:` fixed 3/4-bit bridge vs target-bpw allocator using quant-error, patch-effect, and verifier-saliency scores. `Interpretability hook:` log achieved bpw, selected high-bit atoms, low-bit failure examples, and stability across seeds.

## Highest-priority ablations

1. **LatentMAS competitor harness.** Clone/run LatentMAS-style single-agent, text-MAS, and latent-MAS controls on the same math/code slices as LatentWire; compare accuracy, output tokens, latency, and traceability. Sources: [LatentMAS](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20639), [LatentMAS GitHub](https://github.com/Gen-Verse/LatentMAS).
2. **Routed projector bank.** Replace one bridge with a small bank of low-rank projectors routed by query/layer/confidence; test whether multimodal connector routing transfers to cross-model latent exchange. Sources: [SEMI/projector note](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/unified-sequence-modeling-with-modality-specific-adapters), [cross-modality projector note](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/cross-modality-projector-mlp).
3. **Universal dictionary bridge.** Encode both models through a shared SAE dictionary, transport sparse feature IDs/weights, and decode target-side; compare to raw-coordinate and Procrustes bridges. Sources: [Universal SAEs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.03714), [SAE universal feature spaces](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.06981).
4. **Stitch plus residual repair.** Use model stitching as an initialization/control, then add a tiny target-side correction head only for residual failures. Source: [model stitching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.06609).
5. **Iterative latent refinement.** Add two-to-four bridge refinement steps with adaptive stopping, measuring whether refinement fixes bridge errors or just adds verifier overfit. Sources: [LaDiR](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04573), [FS-DFM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.20624), [progressive refinement](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.04514).
6. **Tokenizer-aware bridge.** Compare token-ID, byte-span, TokAlign-style remap, aligned-logit distillation, and adaptive-vocab compression before latent exchange. Sources: [TokAlign](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.03523), [cross-tokenizer ALM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.20083), [aligned-logit KD](https://aclanthology.org/2025.findings-acl.419/), [AdaptiVocab](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.19693).
7. **Outlier-aware protected frontier.** Add sink-token and grouped-head rotation features to the existing quant-error and exact-patch frontier selectors. Sources: [RotateKV](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.16383), [SpinQuant](https://proceedings.iclr.cc/paper_files/paper/2025/hash/e5b1c0d4866f72393c522c8a00eed4eb-Abstract-Conference.html), [KurTail](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.01483).
8. **Mixed-bit route-atom allocator.** Target a fixed average bpw and allocate high precision to atoms/channels by quant-error, exact-patch score, and verifier saliency. Sources: [EXL2](https://github.com/SinanAkkoyun/exllama-v2), [AWQ](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.00978), [QuaRot](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.00456).
9. **Transport plus linear correction.** Compare bridge-only transport against Hadamard/rotation transport plus a learned linear correction residual. Sources: [KVLinC](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.05373), [TurboQuant PDF](https://openreview.net/attachment?id=tO3ASKZlok&name=pdf).
10. **Asymmetric K/V and vector-quantized bridge.** Test K-only, V-only, asymmetric K/V bit allocation, scalar quantization, and vector quantization under matched byte budgets. Sources: [TurboQuant blog](https://research.google/blog/turboquant-redefining-ai-efficiency-with-extreme-compression/), [TurboAngle](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.27467), [outlier token tracing](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.631/).

## Immediate paper-use guidance

- Use **LatentMAS** as the direct positive-method competitor and force matched reporting on accuracy, tokens, latency, and traceability.
- Treat **tokenizer mismatch**, **outlier/precision allocation**, and **one-shot bridge irreversibility** as separate blockers; do not collapse them into one adapter ablation.
- Stack the most defensible method path as: tokenizer-aware byte/span interface, feature or stitch initialization, protected mixed-bit frontier, and target-side iterative residual repair.
- Keep reviewer-facing interpretability tied to telemetry: feature IDs, patch-effect correlation, bit allocation, route gate entropy, and per-step refinement help/harm.
