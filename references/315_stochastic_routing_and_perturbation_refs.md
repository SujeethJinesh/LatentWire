# Stochastic Routing and Perturbation References

Primary-source memo for the hypothesis that `random/random` KV routing beat deterministic `attention/energy` on GSM30 because stochastic selection is acting like cheap ensemble exploration, uncertainty-aware routing, and perturbation-robust cache transport rather than a pure saliency estimator.

The working assumption is not that randomness is inherently better. The hypothesis is that, under a small budget and a brittle source-target pair, stochastic routing may preserve more useful hypothesis diversity than a deterministic score that is miscalibrated on this task.

## What seems most plausible for GSM30

- Deterministic `attention/energy` likely overfits brittle saliency on a tiny eval slice, while `random/random` behaves like a low-cost ensemble that preserves more candidate hypotheses.
- The useful signal may be in route diversity, not in route optimality. If so, a small amount of stochasticity plus verification can outperform a single deterministic selector.
- If `random/random` keeps winning, the next question is whether it is because of exploration, calibration, or hidden compute allocation. Those should be separated explicitly.

## Highest-priority LatentWire ablations

- `random/random` vs `attention/energy` vs `topk/topk` KV routing with 4/8 stochastic samples and a matched verifier.
- Uncertainty-triggered routing: deterministic when confidence is high, stochastic when confidence is low.
- Multi-route fusion: single stochastic route vs 3-route vote vs oracle-best route under the same byte budget.
- Inference-time KV dropout / cache masking versus deterministic cache reuse, with variance and calibration logging.
- Draft/verifier split: stochastic draft routing followed by a frozen target-model verifier, measured by acceptance rate and GSM accuracy per wall-clock.

## Guardrails for interpretation

- Match bytes, active layers, and wall-clock before comparing deterministic and stochastic routes.
- Log route entropy, disagreement, and seed variance for every stochastic run.
- Treat any win as provisional until it is reproduced on at least one additional GSM slice or source-target pair.
